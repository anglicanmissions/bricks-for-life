Amlaku Yaregal lost his mother at birth and was raised by his sister. At 12, he ran away to pursue an education, living on the streets to survive.

Eventually, he became a guide in Lalibela, where he met Ric Foxley. His faith-filled question became the seed for Bricks for Life.

Ric and others funded Amlaku’s tertiary studies in Plant Science, so that Amlaku could return home to Baregota and teach vital agriculture skills.

Today, the whole community has committed to his techniques. He is now training farmers in neighbouring villages, scaling the impact across the region.

FAQ.

Are donations tax deductible?

Yes. Bricks for Life is run by AWA (Aid & Development), making it eligible for tax credits. Receipts are issued automatically.

How much goes to the project?

90% of your donation goes directly towards the project infrastructure and local labor. 10% is retained for operational oversight.
