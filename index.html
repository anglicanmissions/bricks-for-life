Roots in the
Highlands.

Ethiopia is the birthplace of some of the world's most vital crops. In the village of Baregota, farming isn't just an industry—it is the heartbeat of the community.

However, traditional methods often struggle with seasonal variability. By combining ancestral knowledge with modern science, we are ensuring this legacy continues for generations.

The Journey

Chapter by Chapter.

01

The Runaway Student

Amlaku Yaregal escaped a life on the streets at age 12 to pursue an education. He eventually earned a degree in Plant Science, turning his personal struggle into a community solution.

02

Closing the Hunger Gap

Returning to Baregota, Amlaku replaced traditional broadcasting with precise row-planting and concrete irrigation systems, tripling the village's seasonal yield.

03

The Ripple Effect

The "bricks" of knowledge don't stop at Baregota. Amlaku now trains farmers from surrounding villages, ensuring the entire region can build a future of food security.
