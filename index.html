The Founder's Journey

From the Streets to Plant Science

The Runaway Student

At age 12, Amlaku Yaregal made a choice that would change Baregota forever. He ran away to pursue an education, living on the streets and doing whatever it took to learn. He eventually secured a place at university to study Plant Science.

The Homecoming

Rather than seeking a life of comfort elsewhere, Amlaku returned to his village. He realized that the "hunger gap" (seasonal food shortages) could be solved with the right "bricks"—the foundations of modern farming and water management.

Concrete Outcomes.

How your support translates into tangible change in the village of Baregota.

~500
Villagers Impacted

Water Security

Installed concrete piping and 5000L water tanks. By moving water through pipes instead of open ditches, we stop 30% loss from evaporation.

Crop Science

Transitioned from "broadcasting" seeds to row-planting. This increases yields significantly and ensures food is available year-round.

Economic Agency

Villagers now have a 'surplus' to sell. They aren't just surviving; they are entering the local economy as suppliers.

New Enterprise Launch

The Baregota
Soap Initiative.

Amlaku isn't stopping at food. He is launching a local soap-making business to address two critical needs: **Hygiene and Women’s Empowerment.**

Hygiene Access

Reducing disease and promoting feminine health through affordable, locally-made soap.

Sustainable Revenue

Profits from soap sales are reinvested into the community's agricultural funds.
