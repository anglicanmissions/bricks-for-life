In 2011, this request from Amlaku Yaregal to Ric Foxley initiated a collaborative project focused on agricultural science and regional infrastructure.

Operational
Architecture.

A three-phase approach to community-led sustainability.

01

Agricultural Systems

Optimizing local production through the implementation of row-planting techniques and high-yield seed selection.

02

Hydrological Infrastructure

Construction of a 3.5km concrete canal network to minimize water loss from seepage and evaporation in highland conditions.

03

Economic Enterprise

Diversifying local revenue streams through small-scale manufacturing and the establishment of independent supply chains.

Environmental
Parameters.

The Northern Highlands present unique topographical challenges. At 2,400 meters, agricultural viability depends on precision resource management.

Baregota's project model addresses these variables through scientific plant education and durable civil engineering.

Implementation FAQ.

Resource Allocation

90 percent of contributions are directed toward project materials and local wages. Operational overhead is maintained at 10 percent.

Sustainability Model

The project prioritizes "Agency over Aid," establishing the foundational knowledge required for independent community management.
