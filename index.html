Amlaku lost his mother at birth and was raised by his sister. At 12, he ran away to seek education.

Living on the streets, he eventually became a guide in Lalibela. Meeting Ric Foxley, his faith-filled question became the seed for Bricks for Life.

Funded for a Plant Science degree, Amlaku returned home to teach vital agriculture and irrigation skills.

Today, he trains farmers in neighbouring villages, scaling the impact across the region.

FAQ.

Are donations tax deductible?

Yes. Bricks for Life is managed by AWA (Aid & Development), making it eligible for tax credits. Receipts are generated automatically.

Deployment of Funds

90% of funds reach project infrastructure and local labor. 10% is retained strictly for management and operational oversight.
