Baregota’s altitude requires a specific architecture of farming. We blend ancestral respect with modern water conservation to triple yields.

The Student.

Amlaku Yaregal fled homelessness at age 12 to earn a degree in Plant Science. His return home marks the beginning of a community-wide transformation.

02

The Science.

Row-planting and water storage foundations converted seasonal hunger into year-round independence.

03

The Ripple.

Knowledge scales. Amlaku now mentors farmers from surrounding districts, creating a regional success model.

Irrigation

Concrete piping foundations that stop water loss and enable year-round farming.

Agronomy

Precision row-planting and crop science that triples yield compared to traditional methods.

Agency

Self-sufficiency models that allow the community to enter the market as suppliers.

The Soap
Initiative.

Beyond agriculture, Amlaku is launching a local soap enterprise to tackle hygiene and provide economic agency to the women of Baregota.
